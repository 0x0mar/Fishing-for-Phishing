    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2011/02/25/art-from-everyday-objects-paper-napkins/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Art From Everyday Objects: Paper Napkins">Art From Everyday Objects: Paper Napkins</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

		on		February 25, 2011,
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">

    <div class="post-content"><p><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/blog_artfromeveryday.gif" rel="image_group"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1882" title="blog_artfromeveryday" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/blog_artfromeveryday.gif" alt="" width="571" height="124" /></a></p>
<p>In this installment of Art From Everyday Objects, I&#8217;m working with paper napkins. <a href="http://www.zuhauseingermany.blogspot.com/" target="_blank">Juliette</a> wrote me and said, <em>&#8220;We made the switch to cloth napkins before finishing off our paper  napkin supply, so I’d like to move those out to avoid temptation!&#8221;</em></p>
<p>Juliette, I have several ideas for you! And whoa, who knew paper napkins could serve a purpose more than wiping morsels from messy faces?</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1934" title="paper1" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/paper1.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="761" /></p>
<p>Paper napkins turned into flowers. Just lay out 3 or 4 napkins flat, accordion fold them and then tie a  twist tie around the center. Then fluff them out by peeling apart the  layers. This creates a bow shape. The flower centerpiece above was created using two bows layered  on top of each other. The bows below are made the same way.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1937" title="paper3" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/paper3.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="381" /></p>
<p>Bows! Follow the tutorial above and just wrap around a piece of napkin to cover the twist tie in the center and hot glue.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1935" title="paper2" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/paper2.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="381" /></p>
<p>Paper napkins turned into more flowers. The &#8220;bubbles&#8221; were made using string tied around cotton balls. Similar to <a href="../2010/02/23/easy-diy-accessories/">this concept</a> I created a while ago.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1936" title="paper2a" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/paper2a.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="778" /></p>
<p>I could see a bunch of these covering a wall for a party or covering a  canvas creating a multi-dimensional art piece. Best of all, they  literally only cost pennies to make and take only minutes.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1938" title="paper4" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/paper4.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="380" /></p>
<p>And fluffy pom pom thingys. Made from different size bows and just layered on top of each other.</p>
<p>There you go, 4 unique and pretty ways to use up paper napkins. Do you like? Can you all think of any other ideas?</p>

    <div class="post-content"><p>This is my third attempt at oil painting. You can see my first <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2011/01/20/my-first-oil-painting/">here</a>. I&#8217;m not going to even bother showing you my second. It was a great lesson in creating art, but abandoning crap. More on that later.</p>

<p>I think next week I might try a portrait. It would be a challenge, but it&#8217;s time to try new things!</p>

    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2011/02/10/art-from-everyday-objects-paperclips-string/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Art From Everyday Objects: Paperclips + String">Art From Everyday Objects: Paperclips + String</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-date">
		on		February 10, 2011,
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2011/02/10/art-from-everyday-objects-paperclips-string/#comments">204 comments</a>
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->
    </div><!-- END .post-meta -->

    	
    
    <div class="post-content"><p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1882" title="blog_artfromeveryday" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/blog_artfromeveryday.gif" alt="" width="571" height="124" /></p>
<p>So today I thought it would be a fun challenge to work with paperclips. You can&#8217;t get much more mundane than a paperclip! After much thought, I came up with paperclip earrings. If you have paperclips and some pretty cotton string, you can create these beauties. They&#8217;d probably look pretty cool as a necklace too.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1914" title="earrings" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/earrings4.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="761" /></p>
<p>Here is a diagram to show you where to open the paperclip to create this triangular shape.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1907" title="earrings1" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/earrings1.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="246" /></p>
<p>You are basically unbending the clip at all three of the curves. Does that make sense?</p>
<p>Once you&#8217;ve got the triangular shape, you need to glue the ends together. Use a fast drying epoxy or hot glue. Next you can start winding your cotton thread around the wire. I started out using hot glue, then I tried Liquid Stitch and liked it way more. It&#8217;s a permanent, clear drying adhesive that didn&#8217;t cause any bumps of dried hot glue along the way.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1908" title="earrings2" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/earrings2.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="180" /></p>
<h5>Fig 1: Winding the string around // Fig 2:  Starting to wrap the string around the earring, secured with Liquid Stitch</h5>
<p>Once you&#8217;ve wound all the way round and secured with glue, you can then take another length of string and wind around the width of the &#8220;earring&#8221; creating horizontal stripes or you can create a more messy look by creating a &#8220;net&#8221; design (see below).</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1909" title="earrings3" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/earrings3.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="377" /></p>
<p>Then just clamp on a pair of earring hooks. I just stole mine from a couple pairs of earrings I didn&#8217;t wear anymore. The results are beautiful and surprisingly modern. I love how light they are too. I like big earrings, but I don&#8217;t like it when they weigh down my earlobe. I don&#8217;t want them to hang to my knees when I&#8217;m old!</p>
<p>Let me know if you have any questions or I didn&#8217;t explain something well enough. I&#8217;d love to hear or see them if you try it for yourself!</p>

    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2011/02/02/art-from-everyday-objects-matchsticks/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Art from Everyday Objects: Matchsticks">Art from Everyday Objects: Matchsticks</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-date">
		on		February 2, 2011,
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2011/02/02/art-from-everyday-objects-matchsticks/#comments">16 comments</a>
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->
    </div><!-- END .post-meta -->

    	
    
    <div class="post-content"><p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1882" title="blog_artfromeveryday" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/blog_artfromeveryday.gif" alt="" width="571" height="124" /></p>
<p>We are snowed in here, so I had some time to whip up a neat project today for the <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2011/01/28/art-from-everyday-objects-challenge/">Art from Everyday Objects Challenge</a>. This week I&#8217;ll be creating art with&#8230;</p>
<p>Matchsticks! Natalie from <a href="http://onescrappydoctor.blogspot.com/" target="_blank">Scrap Doctor</a> told me,</p>
<blockquote><p>&#8220;I’ve got about a million matchsticks that currently have no purpose.  They’re left over from my advent calendar which I made with the 25  match-boxes. What to do with them all?!!&#8221;</p></blockquote>
<p>Well Natalie, thanks for the question &#8211; I have several ideas to show you and I&#8217;ll start with this one. Mini Matchstick Arrows!</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1881" title="matchstick1" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/matchstick1.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="680" /></p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1888" title="matchstick2" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/matchstick21.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="428" /></p>
<p>Aren&#8217;t they so cute and tiny!? If you have older kids this could be a fun project to do with them. Then you can stick them in a frame or shadow box. OR, you can assemble a tiny bow and shoot candles aflame. Haha! Here&#8217;s how to do &#8216;em.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1883" title="matchsticks1" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/matchsticks1.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="212" /></p>
<p><strong>Step 1:</strong> Paint the stick with acrylic paint in the color you choose. Set aside to dry.<br />
<strong>Step 2:</strong> Fold and cut out the paper shape used for the end of the arrow</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1884" title="matchsticks2" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/matchsticks2.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="212" /></p>
<p><strong>Step 3: </strong>Cut the folded paper in tiny slices, creating fringe, unfold.<br />
<strong>Step 4:</strong> Paint the tip of the arrow white or whatever other color you choose.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1885" title="matchsticks3" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/02/matchsticks3.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="213" /></p>
<p><strong>Step 5:</strong> Paint two thin black lines around the &#8220;point&#8221; of the arrow.<br />
<strong>Step 6:</strong> Hot glue a tiny dab of glue to the paper, stick on the matchstick.</p>
<p>Repeat until you have a whole army of tiny arrows! Stay tuned, I&#8217;ve got several other great projects with matchsticks lined up.</p>
