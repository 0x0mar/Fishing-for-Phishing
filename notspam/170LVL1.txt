    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2011/03/28/baby-shower-no-8/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Baby Shower No. 8">Baby Shower No. 8</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

		on		March 28, 2011,
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2011/03/28/baby-shower-no-8/#comments">5 comments</a>

    <div class="post-content"><p>I think that&#8217;s how many baby showers I&#8217;ve helped plan in the last 5 years. I love baby showers! There are a few ideas I wanted to share from the most recent one I threw for a friend.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2096" title="shower2" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/shower2.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="381" /></p>
<p><strong>Watercolor BABY sign.</strong> It&#8217;s easy. Just print out <a href="http://www.evie-s.com/downloads/babysign.pdf" target="_blank">this template</a> I created, cut out the letters, trace them onto watercolor paper. Paint your traced letters with watercolors. Doesn&#8217;t have to expensive paint, I just used my 2-year olds! Cut them in circles and hang onto ribbon.     <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2011/03/11/can-art-change-the-world/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Can Art Change the World?">Can Art Change the World?</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-date">
		on		March 11, 2011,
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2011/03/11/can-art-change-the-world/#comments">7 comments</a>
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->
    </div><!-- END .post-meta -->

    	
    
    <div class="post-content"><p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1972" title="art" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/art.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="381" /></p>
<p>I happened upon this <a href="http://www.hulu.com/watch/221157/tedtalks-jrs-ted-prize-wish-use-art-to-turn-the-world-inside-out" target="_blank">TED talk on hulu by artist, JR</a>.  Intrigued by the title, <em>&#8220;Use Art to Turn the World Inside Out,&#8221;</em> I watched the short video and was so utterly inspired by it I just had to share. <a href="http://jr-art.net/" target="_blank">JR is an artist</a> that got his start writing his name everywhere in the cities of Paris as a graffiti artist. After finding a camera in the Paris subway, he started taking portraits of people with interesting stories and pasting them on walls, buildings, roofs and just about everywhere else. In the talk he poses the question, &#8220;Could art change the world?&#8221;</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1973" title="art1" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/art11.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="381" /></p>
<p>In 2007, he did a project called Face 2 Face, the biggest illegal art  exhibition ever. JR posted huge portraits of Israelis and Palestinians  face to face in eight Palestinian and Israeli cities, and on the both  sides of the Security fence / Separation wall. The experts said it would  be impossible. Still, he did it. And people realized in the midst of their conflict, that these people were not so different.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1975" title="art3" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/art3.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="323" /></p>
<p>In 2008, he embarked on a long international trip for women, a  project in which he underlines the dignity of women who are often the  targets of conflicts called, Women Are Heroes. The men in the community would pay tribute to the women in their society by posting their photos on houses.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1976" title="art4" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/art4.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="381" /></p>
<p>This is a picture of the Women are Heroes project in Kibera, Kenya installed in February 2009.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1977" title="art4b" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/art4b.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="323" /></p>
<p>When you look at Kibera now, they look back. (Photo from Google Earth)</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1974" title="art2" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/art2.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="323" /></p>
<p>I particularly love this idea that they created in India. Instead of pasting a photo, they just made the art white and sticky. It looks like just a blank white wall until&#8230;</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1978" title="art2a" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/art2a.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="323" /></p>
<p>dust from a crowded market blows in the air, or in the case of the photo above, the <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2009/04/23/festival-of-colors/">Holi festival of colors</a> is celebrated, then the art is revealed.</p>
<p>JR remains anonymous  for the most part and doesn&#8217;t explain his portraits in detail &#8211; he leaves the space empty for an  encounter between the subject/protagonist and the passer-by/interpreter. This is what JR is working on&#8230;raising questions.</p>
<p>Behind each image is a story. JR is essentially a storyteller. The portraits in and of themselves aren&#8217;t the point, but it&#8217;s what you do with the images and the statement it makes when it&#8217;s pasted. In the talk, JR says,</p>
<blockquote><p>&#8220;In some way art can change the world. Art is not supposed to change the world, to change practical things. But to change the perceptions. Art can change the way we see the world. Art can create energy. Actually, the fact that art cannot change things makes it a neutral place for exchanges and discussions, and then enables you to change the world. What we see changes who we are.&#8221;</p></blockquote>
<p>So what do you think? Can art change the world? I think it can. And I&#8217;m passionate about finding ways to do it.</p>
<div id="_mcePaste" style="position: absolute; left: -10000px; top: 0px; width: 1px; height: 1px; overflow: hidden;">Intrigued by the title, &#8220;Use Art to Turn the World Inside Out.&#8221;</div>

    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2011/03/05/diy-confetti-earrings/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to DIY Confetti Earrings">DIY Confetti Earrings</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

    <div class="post-content"><p>I&#8217;ve been asked a few times where I get my ideas from. While creative ideas can be sometimes be elusive, sometimes they hit me instantly. The other night I was actually working on another project involving a hole puncher &#8211; the kind that has a little reservoir to catch the punched paper. When I opened the plastic hatch and dumped out the dots, they formed a really fantastic linear shape &#8211; random, but still linear. Instead of instantly sweeping them aside, I paused and stared at them for a bit. The arrangement just looked really pretty. Then the idea hit me that it would make a beautiful piece of jewelry if I could do it. So I did it! And I thought I&#8217;d share this with you because it was a great lesson in how a creative idea is formed. It wasn&#8217;t  just a pile of confetti. It&#8217;s art that can be used, transformed,  or manipulated into something else.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1956" title="confetti1" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/confetti1.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="855" /></p>
<p>Can you believe it&#8217;s just paper? They are delicate, no doubt. I wouldn&#8217;t jump into a swimming pool with them on, but if you baby them, they should hold up decently. But who cares if they only last one wearing. They are awesome and practically free.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1957" title="confetti2" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/confetti2.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="444" /></p>
<p>It&#8217;s like a party on your ears!</p>
<p>All you need is a hole punch, some pretty paper (the thicker the better, as long as your hole punch will go through it), hot glue, really thin wire (I used 32 gauge) and some earring hooks.</p>
<p><strong>Step 1:</strong> Punch out your circles. I used 9-10 circles for mine. (A friend of mine suggested using shellac or spray varnish on the dots to make them for longer wear.)</p>
<p><strong>Step 2:</strong> Using just tiny dabs of hot glue, start arranging and glueing your dots together in a random, but linear fashion. You want them to look like they are falling.</p>
<p><strong>Step 3:</strong> Clip a small piece of wire and hot glue onto your bottom circle. This is what will connect the final dangling circle to give it the illusion of falling.</p>
<p><strong>Step 4:</strong> Hot glue the final dot to the other end of the wire.</p>
<p><strong>Step 5:</strong> Attach your earring hooks by punching a tiny hole in your finished piece with a straight pin. I&#8217;m not a jeweler, so there may be a better way to do it, but I just latched mine directly onto the hook.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1958" title="confetti3" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/confetti3.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="855" /></p>
<p>That&#8217;s it! Sorry I don&#8217;t have any step-by-step photos of the process. But if you have questions, just leave them in the comments!</p>
