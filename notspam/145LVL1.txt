    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2009/01/30/im-knitting-flowers/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to I&#8217;m Knitting Flowers">I&#8217;m Knitting Flowers</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-date">
		on		January 30, 2009,
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2009/01/30/im-knitting-flowers/#comments">3 comments</a>
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->
    </div><!-- END .post-meta -->

    	
    
    <div class="post-content"><p>There is something about the cold lately that made me get out my knitting needles and start stitching up a small mountain of cozy, yarn-y creations! I knit several of these flowers that I&#8217;ll probably end up making a pin out of, or sewing them on something. You could also make up a bunch of these and create a pretty garland!</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-349" title="Knit Flowers" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2009/01/knitflowers.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="218" /></p>
<p>They are super easy to make. Here is the pattern:</p>
<blockquote><p>I used a US size #8 needle.<br />
Cast on 8 stitches.<br />
Row 1: slip 1 purlwise, knit 7<br />
Row 2: slip 1 knitwise, knit 5 (2 sts remain on left needle), turn and put yarn to the back.<br />
Row 3: slip 1 knitwise, knit 3 (2 sts remain on left needle), turn and put yarn to the back.<br />
Row 4: slip 1 knitwise, knit 3, turn.<br />
Row 5: slip 1 knitwise, knit 5 (0 sts remain on left needle), turn.<br />
Row 6: slip 1 knitwise, knit 6 (leaving 1 st on left needle), turn.<br />
Row 7: slip 1 knitwise and begin casting off until 1 st remains, turn.<br />
Cast on 7 stitches. Repeat 5 times to create 6 petals.<br />
Cast off last sts leaving a long tail. Thread yarn on a wool sewing needle and weave sewing needle in and out of each petal along inside edge of hold and gently pull yarn together, closing hole.</p></blockquote>
