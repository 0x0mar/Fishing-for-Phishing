<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-516" title="craftsbanner2" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2009/05/craftsbanner2.jpg" alt="craftsbanner2" width="571" height="213" /></p>

Then I used a scalloped rotary cutter to create edges around the design using Martha&#8217;s tool from her craft line. I also used her paper and shape punch to create the starburst. Then I put it all together and took a photograph. Once I got a photo I liked, then I went into Photoshop and added some more details.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-515" title="Crafts Design Submission" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2009/05/craftsbanner.jpg" alt="Crafts Design Submission" width="571" height="139" /></p>
<p>I&#8217;m happy with the result and would be thrilled to win, but I&#8217;m not getting my hopes up. It would be cool to win the Encyclopedia of Crafts book though.</p>
<p>You can see full-size photos of the project <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/evie-s/3549606228/" target="_blank">here</a>. I&#8217;d love to know what you think of it!</p>

    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2009/05/08/creative-moms-ceeb-wassermann/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Creative Moms: Ceeb Wassermann">Creative Moms: Ceeb Wassermann</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2009/05/08/creative-moms-ceeb-wassermann/#comments">2 comments</a>
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->
    </div><!-- END .post-meta -->

    	
    
    <div class="post-content"><p>Our final interview in the Creative Moms series is with jeweler, <a href="http://ceebwassermann.com/home.html" target="_blank">Ceeb Wassermann</a>, owner of Mettle Design Studio. I had the opportunity to highlight some of Ceeb&#8217;s beautiful jewelry <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2008/03/26/floral-inspired-jewelry-by-ceeb-wassermann/" target="_blank">last year</a> and since then have built up a small friendship online. She takes inspiration from her extensive experience in gold and silversmithing. Trained in Australia, Germany and Switzerland, her work has been exhibited in Sydney, LA, NYC, Zurich and Berlin, and is now available on <a href="http://www.etsy.com/shop.php?user_id=73585" target="_blank">her etsy shop</a>. Here is her interview.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-459" title="ceeb" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2009/05/ceeb.jpg" alt="ceeb" width="571" height="155" /></p>
<p><strong>1. First, tell us a little bit about yourself.</strong><br />
I&#8217;m half Japanese, half German, Brazilian born and currently living in Sydney, Australia. I have been a jeweler for  over 20 years. I also have a visuals arts degree with a major in jewelry and object design and I have also done one in psychology (very common amongst artists, i think). I&#8217;m a mom with a 10-year old (10 going on 35), who is  very musical. I absolutely adore metal and Japanese design and of course I love food! I watch a lot of food shows when I get a chance, but I don&#8217;t cook, I make jewelry instead.</p>
<p><strong>2. Just about everything came to a complete stop and I had to shift my priorities when I had my son 6 months ago. What did you learn along the way about starting a business while raising a child?</strong><br />
You have to be fairly good at multitasking and as passionate about your business as you are about your child/children/family.  I found that I slept a lot less and stretched myself in many ways that I would not have imagined before having a child.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-464" title="ceeb11" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2009/05/ceeb11.jpg" alt="ceeb11" width="571" height="143" /></p>
<h4>From left to right: <a href="http://www.etsy.com/view_listing.php?listing_id=7954466" target="_blank">Field of Flowers Cuff</a> // <a href="http://www.etsy.com/view_listing.php?listing_id=24551857" target="_blank">Ayame Pendant</a> // <a href="http://www.etsy.com/view_listing.php?listing_id=22699138" target="_blank">Kiyo Wheat Sheaf Pendant</a></h4>
<p><strong>3. Have you taught your daughter the jeweling craft? Does she have any interest in the creative arts like you?</strong><br />
<a href="http://saschaK.etsy.com" target="_blank">My daughter</a> does some work for me whenever she wants to earn extra money. She can do quite a lot of the production work that doesn&#8217;t involve any fire or dangerous chemicals or heavy machinery. But essentially she is a drummer &#8211; that is what she is aiming for and she&#8217;s pretty good at it. Jewelry is a &#8216;back up&#8217; for her.</p>
<p><strong>4. What keeps you inspired and motivated to create while still being a mom?</strong><br />
I&#8217;m very passionate about my work. If I didn&#8217;t make anything, I think that I would lose motivation to get up in the morning. Kind of like a domino effect. So that keeps me passionate about all the &#8216;mom&#8217; stuff too. My daughter and I are great friends. We do alot of &#8216;road trips&#8217; together and sketch and find interesting things on our travels. She&#8217;s a whiz at reading maps!</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-461" title="ceeb2" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2009/05/ceeb2.jpg" alt="ceeb2" width="571" height="241" /></p>
<p><strong>5. How do you maintain a balance between motherhood and the work you do?</strong><br />
When my daughter was younger I was a part-time mom and a part-time jeweler. Now there has been a shift over a few years as she doesn&#8217;t need me as much as she used to, which gives me more time to put into my work.</p>
<p><strong>6. Who are some of your favorite artists?</strong><br />
<a href="http://basement.craftaustralia.org.au/LivingTreasures/20071111.php" target="_blank">margaret west</a><br />
<a href="http://www.sheridankennedy.com/" target="_blank">sheridan kennedy</a><br />
<a href="http://www.powerhousemuseum.com/collection/database/?irn=140880" target="_blank">rowena gough</a><br />
<a href="http://www.galleryfunaki.com.au/gf/artists/29/carlier-makigawa" target="_blank">carlier makigawa</a><br />
<a href="http://www.pablofanque.com.au/?page_id=48 " target="_blank">richard burns</a></p>
<p>There are many more. But the list would be immense.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-465" title="ceeb31" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2009/05/ceeb31.jpg" alt="ceeb31" width="571" height="143" /></p>
<h4>From left to right: <a href="http://www.etsy.com/view_listing.php?listing_id=23733573" target="_blank">Matisse Inspired Seaweed Pendant</a> // <a href="http://www.etsy.com/view_listing.php?listing_id=24116350" target="_blank">Little Sakura Earrings</a> //<a href="http://www.etsy.com/view_listing.php?listing_id=24376051" target="_blank">Chrysanthemum Pendant</a></h4>
<p><strong>7. What are your mother’s day wishes?</strong><br />
A bit of a cliche for an answer: healthy, happy life and random acts of kindness and no arthritis in my hands.</p>
<p>&#8212;-</p>
<p>Thank you so much Ceeb! It&#8217;s a great reminder that you need to have the passion for your craft in order to make it work while still being a mom. You are an inspiration, and we wish you even more success in your business!</p>
<p>That wraps up our series. I hope you all enjoyed it and if you are a mom, Happy Mother&#8217;s Day! You rock.</p>

    <div class="post-content"><p>Our next interview in the Creative Moms series is with artist, photographer, and etsy store owner, Lisa Ensor of <a href="http://www.etsy.com/shop.php?user_id=7118926" target="_blank">Lizzy Brookes Handmade</a>. But my true love is shopping at the flea market and making things by hand. After graduating with a Studio Art Degree, I began teaching. I would learn new mediums of art, just so I could teach them: knitting, calligraphy, acrylic painting etc. I&#8217;ve recently become very interested in textiles as well.</p>
<p><strong>2. What do you do as an artist?</strong><br />
I still do a little photography on the side, mainly for people I know. But I love embellishing shirts and sweaters with vintage accents! I sew, embroider, paint and knit on a fairly regular basis. I also photograph my products for my etsy shop.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-426" title="lizzybrookes2" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2009/05/lizzybrookes2.jpg" alt="lizzybrookes2" width="571" height="273" /></p>

I also look at Anthropologie catalogues (I save all of them), their website and I read a few blogs that keep me motivated and inspired.</p>
<p><strong>4. How do you maintain a balance between motherhood and the work you do?</strong><br />
I&#8217;ve always wanted to be one of those women who do certain things on certain days at specific times. Unfortunately I&#8217;m a some what scatter-brained artist. I get in these modes- the cleaning mode, the just-want-to-play mode and the business mode. Finding the balance everyday is the challenge. Every morning I try to wake up early, make coffee, read my Bible and make a to do list; I always want to get the list done- but having fun and enjoying my life is more important to me. I try to work on my business Lizzy Brookes handmade and the new Lizzy Brookes Vintage while Brooklyn is napping. I have to work quickly and work smart during those daily naps!</p>

<a href="http://www.alysonfox.com/" target="_blank">Alyson Fox&#8217;s illustrations, photographs and fashion</a> &#8211; Alyson&#8217;s illustrations were featured in a magazine and since then I regularly look through her photographs and illustrations.<br />
<a href="http://www.maxwanger.com" target="_blank">Max Wagner&#8217;s photography</a> &#8211; Joanna featured Max on her blog cup of Jo and I love his organic and ethereal photographic style.<br />
<a href="http://www.anthropologie.com" target="_blank">Anthropologie</a> &#8211; My favorite store in the world! What girl doesn&#8217;t love clothes? But I LOVE the fabric, the design and seeing the color combinations, as well as the beautiful photography in Anthropologie.<br />
I also peruse etsy&#8217;s collections of art, vintage clothes and my boxes full of vintage clothes and embellishments!<br />
I love these etsy shops: <a href="http://thelittlestitch.etsy.com">thelittlestitch.etsy.com</a> and <a href="http://13bees.etsy.com" target="_blank">13bees.etsy.com</a> and <a href="http://bluemango.etsy.com" target="_blank">bluemango.etsy.com</a>.</p>

    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2009/05/06/creative-moms-ez-of-creature-comforts/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Creative Moms: Ez of Creature Comforts">Creative Moms: Ez of Creature Comforts</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-date">
		on		May 6, 2009,
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2009/05/06/creative-moms-ez-of-creature-comforts/#comments">7 comments</a>
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->
    </div><!-- END .post-meta -->

    	
    
    <div class="post-content"><p>This week I&#8217;m debuting a series of interviews in honor of Mother&#8217;s Day, called Creative Moms. It&#8217;s an opportunity to hear from other artsy moms out there on how they juggle their work, and creativity, all while being a stellar mom.</p>
<p>We kick off the series interviewing Ez of the design and style blog, <a href="http://creaturecomforts.typepad.com" target="_blank">Creature Comforts</a>. Ez has generously given us her time to answer a few questions relating to being a creative mom. She runs <a href="http://www.etsy.com/shop.php?user_id=5253898" target="_blank">not just one</a>, but <a href="http://www.etsy.com/shop.php?user_id=7172822" target="_blank">two etsy shops</a> and her blog has thousands of followers. With an eye for beauty, she&#8217;s one of the sweetest people I&#8217;ve had the privilege to meet via the blogosphere. Here is her interview.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-453" title="creaturecomforts4" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2009/05/creaturecomforts4.jpg" alt="creaturecomforts4" width="571" height="155" /></p>
<p><strong>1. First, tell us a little bit about yourself.</strong><br />
It&#8217;s always tricky talking about oneself, but I guess I could say firstly that I&#8217;m a mom (to a wonderful 10 year old) and also that I&#8217;m a lover of beauty found in unexpected places.  I write my blog Creature Comforts full time and also take photographs and run two little shops on the side.  It&#8217;s a busy life but a good one, and I&#8217;m thankful for it every day.</p>
<p><strong>2. My son is only 6 months, but I look forward to doing creative projects when he gets a little older. Is there anything of note you did with your daughter when she was young to pass on your appreciation for art and design?</strong><br />
Ever since my daughter was a wee thing I&#8217;ve always made an effort to let her be involved in whatever craft or activity I was working on at the moment.  This has posed challenges for me, as I am a bit of a perfectionist and I&#8217;ve had to learn to be okay with cookies that were overdone or paint spilling on the carpet, etc.  One thing that I say often now, is that there are no mistakes in art&#8230; only opportunities to try something different.  This has helped to ease the pressure I think we can feel when we hold ourselves to an unreasonable expectations of &#8220;perfection&#8221;&#8230; and has taught us both to love things in every form&#8230;even the &#8220;less than ideal.&#8221;</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-445" title="creaturecomforts2" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2009/05/creaturecomforts2.jpg" alt="creaturecomforts2" width="571" height="194" /></p>
<h4>From left to right: <a href="http://www.etsy.com/view_listing.php?listing_id=24140204" target="_blank">The Ranunculus Gathering</a> // <a href="http://www.etsy.com/view_listing.php?ref=vl_other_1&amp;listing_id=24140115" target="_blank">Springs First Blush</a> //  <a href="http://www.etsy.com/view_listing.php?listing_id=15968638" target="_blank">Imitation Is &#8211; Diptych &#8211; Photographic Print</a></h4>
<p><strong>3. Does she have any emerging art talents?</strong><br />
I think all parents think their children are talented, and I&#8217;m certainly no different.  I guess what I love most about the creative side of my daughter is that she is not afraid to try new things.  She is always dabbling in some project or other.  While I would love for her to pursue an artistic career-path, her current passion is for acting.  She is ridiculously funny, dramatic and is always creating fantastic skits with costumes, music and the whole kit n&#8217; caboodle.  It&#8217;s a delight to watch her doing something that she loves so much.</p>
<p><strong>4. What keeps you inspired and motivated to create while still being a mom?</strong><br />
Some days are tough&#8230; I&#8217;m not going to lie.  But in the end it is my daughter who inspires me most of all.  I also try to take a few quiet moments to myself each day&#8230; nothing major.  Sometimes it&#8217;s just reading a magazine while sipping tea out of a pretty teacup (you&#8217;d be amazed by how much upgrading from a boring mug to a pretty cup can brighten your day), or a short walk outside to snap a quick photo or two of something floral.  Nurturing ourselves is sometimes the last thing on our to-do lists, but it is so important.  It does take effort to make it happen, but when I set a few minutes aside for myself each day I know that I am a happier person overall, and this effects everything I do in a positive way.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-444" title="creaturecomforts1" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2009/05/creaturecomforts1.jpg" alt="creaturecomforts1" width="571" height="256" /></p>
<p><strong>5. How do you maintain a balance between motherhood and the work you do?</strong><br />
Balance is something I&#8217;m still trying to work on, but over time it&#8217;s getting better.  For me the most important thing is that my daughter knows that even though I&#8217;m working at home (and busy a lot of the time) I&#8217;m always here for her.  We also set aside special time each week to just hang out and have fun together.  Saturdays are generally family day (I try to stay offline as much as possible), and Sunday&#8217;s we have scheduled picnics outside and often take walks around the neighborhood (or play games indoors if it&#8217;s yucky outside).</p>
<p><strong>6. Who are some of your favorite artists and why?</strong><br />
Oh man&#8230; there are so many that I adore.  A few of the first ones that come to mind are:<br />
<a href="http://www.juliemorstad.com/" target="_blank">Julie Morstad</a><br />
<a href="http://www.etsy.com/shop.php?user_id=5016424" target="_blank">Amanda Blake</a><br />
<a href="http://www.ohmycavalier.etsy.com" target="_blank">Julianna Swaney</a><br />
<a href="http://www.etsy.com/shop.php?user_id=5720667" target="_blank">Heather Smith Jones</a><br />
<a href="http://www.hadleyhutton.com/" target="_blank">Hadley Hutton</a><br />
<a href="http://www.etsy.com/shop.php?user_id=64858" target="_blank">Paola Zakimi</a></p>
<p>I own work from all each of these talented ladies and I can&#8217;t get enough.  I feel that there is a certain wistful longing or sadness behind their pieces that really draws me in.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-446" title="creaturecomforts3" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2009/05/creaturecomforts3.jpg" alt="creaturecomforts3" width="571" height="207" /></p>
<h4>From left to right: <a href="http://www.etsy.com/view_listing.php?listing_id=24641407" target="_blank">Bakers Twine &#8211; Red</a> // <a href="http://www.etsy.com/view_listing.php?listing_id=24639896" target="_blank">Bakers Twine &#8211; Brown</a> // <a href="http://www.etsy.com/view_listing.php?listing_id=23889654" target="_blank">white slide box</a></h4>
<p><strong>7. And lastly, what are your mother’s day wishes?</strong><br />
We are moving to New Jersey (from California) in a couple of months and this will probably be the last mother&#8217;s day I will spend with my Mom for a long time, so I&#8217;m wishing that the day will be filled with lots of memory making and fun.   My other wish has already come true&#8230; and that is that I have the privilege of being a mom to the greatest kid in the universe!  Really!!!</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-455" title="creaturecomforts41" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2009/05/creaturecomforts41.jpg" alt="creaturecomforts41" width="571" height="268" /></p>
<h4>These images taken from Ez&#8217;s amazing <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/creature_comforts/sets/" target="_blank">flickr photostream</a>.</h4>
<p>Thank you so much Ez for taking the time for this interview! It&#8217;s so inspiring to hear about how you fit creativity into everyday life.</p>
