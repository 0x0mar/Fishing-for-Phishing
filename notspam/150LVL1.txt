    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-date">

	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">

    </div><!-- END .post-meta -->

    	
    
    <div class="post-content"><p>I don&#8217;t post too much about fashion here (maybe I should start!), but I thought it would be fun to post a couple of the hair accessories I made. The first one is a cheapo plastic headband that I hot glued a feather on. That was easy enough.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-601" title="hair accessories" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/hairaccessories.jpg" alt="hair accessories" width="571" height="230" /></p>
<p>The other one is an idea I had to make a braided fabric headband. Then I saw <a href="http://www.eluckyme.com/home/2009/6/12/diy-headbands.html" target="_blank">this post</a> with a similar idea, and that was  enough motivation for me to do it. Here is what you need:</p>
<ul>
<li>Fabric</li>
<li>Ruler</li>
<li>Scissors</li>
</ul>
<p>Simply cut three strands of fabric about an inch wide from fabric. Then, gather your strands so they are even and tie a knot at the top. Put something heavy on top and start braiding until you get to the end. Then make another knot and tie the finished product around your beautiful head. Sooo easy!</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-600" title="diy hair accessories" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/diy_hairaccessories.jpg" alt="diy hair accessories" width="571" height="433" /></p>
<p>It&#8217;s a quick and easy way to add some flair to your look. To see full-size photos, <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/evie-s/3660171799/" target="_blank">go here</a>.</p>

    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2009/06/11/cracked-wood-bowl-rescue/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Cracked Wood Bowl Rescue">Cracked Wood Bowl Rescue</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-date">
		on		June 11, 2009,
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2009/06/11/cracked-wood-bowl-rescue/#comments">8 comments</a>
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->
    </div><!-- END .post-meta -->

    	
    
    <div class="post-content"><p>I picked up this beautiful solid wood bowl in the FREE pile at an estate sale. Of course I couldn&#8217;t resist picking it up and doing something with it to give it a new life. So I had the idea to fill the crack with some homemade clay. I made the clay, squished it into all the cracks, cleaned off all the excess, and then poked little dots into the clay with a toothpick. Put it into the oven, and that&#8217;s it!</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-559" title="Cracked Wood Bowl" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2009/06/crackedwoodbowl.jpg" alt="Cracked Wood Bowl" width="571" height="404" /></p>
<p>It&#8217;s much more presentable now, and I was lucky that the crack made such a pretty design.</p>
<p>Here is an easy recipe for homemade clay:</p>
<ul>
<li>1 cup flour</li>
<li>1/2 cup salt</li>
<li>1/4 cup water</li>
</ul>
<p>Make sure the dough isn&#8217;t too stiff or too watery. Knead by hand until smooth. Bake at 250 degrees for about an hour and a half.</p>
<p>So the lesson here is, just because something is damaged doesn&#8217;t mean it&#8217;s worthless. Be creative and figure out a way to rescue it! To see full-size photos <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/evie-s/3616849151/" target="_blank">go here</a>.</p>
