    <div class="post-content"><p>Among the many styles of paper crafts, quilling is some what of a rare art form. Quilling, or paper filigree as it is sometimes called, is the art of creating intricate shapes from strips of paper. The technique is simple: After a long, narrow ribbon of paper is rolled around a needle like tool, the strip is slipped off, arranged with your fingertips into the desired shape, and then glued to a background of contrasting paper, fabric, or wood.</p>

    <div class="post-content"><p>If you are anything like me you have a mountain of fabric that is just waiting to be used. Why not use it for gift wrap this Christmas? Here are two ideas:</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-325" title="Fabric Gift Wrap" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2008/12/fabricgiftwrap.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="292" /></p>
<h3>Fabric Gift Bags</h3>
<p>If you love giving gifts and are looking for a way to avoid the waste involved with traditional gift wrap, fabric gift bags just might be the perfect solution.  Not only are they environmentally friendly, but they are completely reusable, oh so easy to use, and beautiful too.  [ <a href="http://agentgenius.com/?p=8162" target="_blank">Via AgentGenius.com</a> ]</p>
<h3>Furoshiki</h3>
<p>The form of wrapping with cloth is a Japanese tradition called Furoshiki. Once used to ease the load in carrying items, it is now often used for gift wrapping, interior decoration and more. It’s very simple, it only takes one square piece, and the colorful scarf can make a great second gift. For all the basic and advanced techniques visit the <a href="http://furoshiki.com/techniques.php" target="_blank">Furoshiki website</a> and experiment! [ <a href="via partly sunny http://partlysunny.ca/2008/01/29/the-art-of-gift-wrapping/" target="_blank">Via Partly Sunny</a> ]</p>
