    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2008/07/28/wicker-chair-redux-for-nursery/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Wicker Chair Redux for Nursery">Wicker Chair Redux for Nursery</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-date">
		on		July 28, 2008,
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2008/07/28/wicker-chair-redux-for-nursery/#comments">1 comment</a>
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->
    </div><!-- END .post-meta -->

    	
    
    <div class="post-content"><p>I&#8217;ve been shopping around for quite some time for the perfect rocking chair for my nursery. Did I mention we are having a boy? Ever since I found that out, I&#8217;ve been searching for everything that would fit a cute, boyish room. When I found this wicker chair, I thought it would fit into my vision perfectly, but it need some work. The sturdy frame desperately needed a coat of fresh paint, and the cushion was hard and covered in 3 layers of dirty, worn fabric. So I set to work to give this chair a new life!</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-285" title="Wicker Chair" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2008/07/wickerchair.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="377" /></p>
<p><strong>Materials:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Wicker Chair &#8211; $25 (purchased from an estate sale)</li>
<li>2 yards of fabric &#8211; $2 (also purchased from an estate sale)</li>
<li>Paint</li>
<li>2 yards of batting for cushion &#8211; $2.50</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Process:</strong></p>
<ol>
<li>Completely repainted the chair with with white paint</li>
<li>Removed old fabric from cushion</li>
<li>Measured and cut batting and new fabric</li>
<li>Attached the fabric and batting to the cushion with a staple gun</li>
</ol>
<p>I&#8217;m super pleased with the final product. Now I just need to sew a cute pillow for some cushioning in the back!</p>
