    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2013/11/17/nature-collection-south-carolina/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Nature Collection: South Carolina">Nature Collection: South Carolina</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

		on		November 17, 2013,
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2013/11/17/nature-collection-south-carolina/#comments">1 comment</a>

    <div class="post-content"><p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>“If you will stay close to nature, to its simplicity, to the small things hardly noticeable, those things can unexpectedly become great and immeasurable.”  -Rainer Maria Rilke <em>(via <a href="http://instagram.com/domesticpoetry">domesticpoetry</a>)</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>On our travels I&#8217;ve been collecting a growing bag of treasures from every place we visit. I don&#8217;t have the heart to just toss them out, so I decided to start a series of photographs of the natural findings from every state. South Carolina was abundant in it&#8217;s beautiful natural fruits &#8211; the leaves were just about to turn so their were a few lucky ones that had already donned their new vibrant hue before it&#8217;s lonely descent towards earth. Rocks and flakes of Mica from the sand, bark from the White Birch, <a href="http://instagram.com/p/fVkSbBqUY4/">exotic wildflowers</a>, bracket fungus, and lovely <a href="http://mountainmoss.com/products-page/nursery/dicranum-mood-windswept-moss-tray/">Dicranum moss</a> were among the found treasures.</p>
<p><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/SouthCarolina1.jpg" rel="image_group"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3638" alt="SouthCarolina" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/SouthCarolina1.jpg" width="602" height="779" /></a></p>
<p>I think it will be a fun project, and maybe I can eventually do this for every state!</p>
<p>We are finishing up our visit in Florida this week while Kent works with the organization, <a href="http://onehope.net/">One Hope</a>. Florida has been beautiful, but I&#8217;ve been really happy to connect with some new friends here during our stay &#8211; it&#8217;s amazing how quickly one can feel isolated and alone without these kind of relationships on the road. Then, on to Kansas City for Thanksgiving!</p>
<p>I&#8217;d love to hear from you in the comments &#8211; what are your Thanksgiving plans?</p>
