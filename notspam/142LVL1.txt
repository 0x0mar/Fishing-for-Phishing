    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2008/09/16/diy-baby-sling/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to DIY Baby Sling">DIY Baby Sling</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-date">
		on		September 16, 2008,
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2008/09/16/diy-baby-sling/#comments">8 comments</a>
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->
    </div><!-- END .post-meta -->

    	
    
    <div class="post-content"><p>So I&#8217;m having a baby soon and I&#8217;m on a budget. I really really really wanted to have a sling, especially for when E is a little newborn. I registered for one, but I didn&#8217;t get it at my shower, so I decided to just make one. Couldn&#8217;t be too hard could it? It wasn&#8217;t.</p>
<p>I found this pattern for the <a href="http://www.mayawrap.com/n_sewsling.php" target="_blank">Maya Wrap</a> and started gathering the materials I needed. The week I actually decided to make one, I found an awesome King size sheet at an estate sale that I loved the pattern of, so I bought it for $2 bucks. Next I went looking for the rings, which I eventually found at our local craft store. I opted for 2 inch diameter rings because I was using much less fabric for a small size sling.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-299" title="DIY Baby Sling" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2008/09/sling.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="242" /></p>
<p>It took some cutting, sewing a few hems and some stitching to sew it together, but it was SO easy and inexpensive. The materials cost me about $3 and it took about 30 minutes to make. Success!</p>
<p>You can see a full-size photo <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/evie-s/2863314067/" target="_blank">here</a>.</p>
