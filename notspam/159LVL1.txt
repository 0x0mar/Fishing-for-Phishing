		<div id="post-1350" class="post-1350 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-about-me category-kids category-motherhood">
    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2010/03/31/wife-mom-artist/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Wife, Mom, Artist">Wife, Mom, Artist</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-date">
		on		March 31, 2010,
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2010/03/31/wife-mom-artist/#comments">13 comments</a>
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->
    </div><!-- END .post-meta -->

    	
    
    <div class="post-content"><p>Seventeen months have passed since I became a mother. A big, shiny new title that&#8217;s been added to my name. I have to say that as a full-time mom it is the most challenging job I have ever had. What makes it more difficult is that I feel an equal need to still be a great wife, and not give up my passion for art and creating. So how do these roles mix? Are they even supposed to mix?</p>
<p>I used to compartmentalize these roles and segment them in a way that felt like structure to me. I work hard to have structure in my life, and would say to myself when the baby wakes up, &#8220;<em>OK, now I&#8217;m a mom, do your thing</em>&#8221; and then when the baby is asleep and it&#8217;s just me, &#8220;<em>now I&#8217;m an artist and this is my time to work or create</em>&#8221; or when I&#8217;m with my husband, &#8220;<em>now I&#8217;m a wife</em>.&#8221;</p>
<p>After a while, I grew tired of this way of thinking and acting because they can&#8217;t (and shouldn&#8217;t) be completely segmented.  With this way of thinking, my need to create as an artist would end up getting frustrated and in turn I would wish away the time spent with my son, awaiting his next nap so I could tackle my next project.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1365" title="whoiam" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/whoiam1.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="246" /></p>
<p><strong>I realized that each major role I have overlap and mesh together to form who I am right  now.</strong> Now that I&#8217;ve had this enlightenment, it makes life a whole lot easier. I choose now to use my creativity to come up with meaningful, creative and fun  ways to spend time together. We color, we play with play doh, we read  books, we explore outside, we play music on the piano and sing (no TV!). I can  say now that I am content when I&#8217;m with my son and I don&#8217;t wish I was  doing anything else.</p>
<p>I look forward to when he gets older so we can do  all kinds of  projects together. I love how my friend Mandy will <a href="http://www.messycanvas.com/2009/12/little-dude-plushie-creativity-with-kids/" target="_blank">take drawings of creatures her kids have created and sew stuffed toys that resemble the drawings</a>.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1356" title="mandy" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/mandy.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="381" /></p>
<p>I love how <a href="http://madebyjoel.blogspot.com" target="_blank">this artist</a> has immersed his kids in creativity and art with amazing handmade toys and art projects.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1355" title="joel" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/joel.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="377" /></p>
<p>So now I no longer watch the clock and live for his next nap time. I&#8217;ve blurred the lines of artistry and motherhood just by loving my time with him and focusing on where my role as a mom and as an artist intersect. Sometimes I can start a project when he&#8217;s awake and then finish it when he&#8217;s asleep. Ultimately, his nap time becomes my focused work time. I always have a mental list of things I need to do when he sleeps and I do what I can in that time.</p>
<blockquote><p>Creativity and motherhood are not separate entities. One does not detract from the other and there is no weighting one by shorting the other. It is not about stealing time for creativity from being a mother and vice versa. Creativity infuses motherhood, and your motherhood colors everything about your creativity.</p></blockquote>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1362" title="IMG_7463" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/IMG_7463.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="356" /></p>
<blockquote><p>Remember, creativity is a holistic approach to life. It is not just about that painting, sewing, writing or whatever it may be, it is about the way you look at things. It is about being open to the adventure. It is about looking for new uses for old things. It is about coming up with solutions to problems that do not seem solvable. It is about seeing beauty and meaning in the mundane. Creativity is about learning to speak what is in your heart. It is about looking for and nurturing possibility. It is about feeding your soul with what nourishes and about sharing with those around you, in effect, feeding them too. And all that? That&#8217;s what it means to raise children, too.</p></blockquote>
</div>

    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2010/03/12/kenya-changed-my-life/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Kenya Changed My Life">Kenya Changed My Life</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-date">
		on		March 12, 2010,
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2010/03/12/kenya-changed-my-life/#comments">14 comments</a>
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->
    </div><!-- END .post-meta -->

    	
    
    <div class="post-content"><p>No, I wasn&#8217;t able to travel 50,000 miles to Kenya with <a href="http://www.churchrelevance.com" target="_blank">my husband</a> 12 days ago. No, I didn&#8217;t see the faces or shake the hands or hear the stories face to face. My feet never touched it&#8217;s soil, but Kenya changed me and it changed my husband.</p>
<p>It was the story of our recently sponsored child, <a href="http://churchrelevance.com/owen/">Owen</a>, who is 8 years old, knows 3 languages and wants to be a pilot someday. We give him $38 a month, and with that money, <a href="http://www.compassion.com" target="_blank">Compassion International</a> gives him an opportunity to not just survive, but thrive. Giving him a chance at an education, and to escape the grip of extreme poverty.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1329" title="owen" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/owen.jpg" alt="" width="540" height="278" /></p>
<h5>Photo by <a href="http://www.keelymariescott.com/" target="_blank">Keely Scott</a></h5>
<p>My husband got to meet Owen, to experience the joy in his face as he took him to a Kenyan amusement park, to hear his &#8220;ahhs&#8221; as he saw photos of me and my son, and watch him marvel at how many toys my son had (there were two toys in the photo my husband showed him).</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1334" title="kenya1" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/kenya11.jpg" alt="" width="540" height="405" /></p>
<h5>Photo by <a href="http://www.keelymariescott.com/" target="_blank">Keely Scott</a></h5>
<p>It was hearing the quote by <a href="http://shaungroves.com/" target="_blank">Shaun Groves</a> that:</p>
<blockquote><p>“Compassion International is trying to help release the poor from poverty, but it’s also <strong>helping to release us from our wealth</strong>.”</p></blockquote>
<p>It was reading the stories of women like <a href="http://churchrelevance.com/get-gobsmacked-save-babies/" target="_blank">Eunice</a>. Right now I&#8217;m wearing the necklace that my husband brought back for me that was made by a woman that has been taught by Compassion&#8217;s <a href="http://rescuebabiesnow.org/" target="_blank">Child Survival Program</a>.  She rolls slivers of old posters, calendars, and scrap paper into beads coated with varnish.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1331" title="necklace" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/necklace.jpg" alt="" width="540" height="359" /></p>
<p>These mothers are full of so much joy that they have been taught to do something that can provide for the daily needs of their family.</p>
<p>It was being moved by these amazing people who have so little, but yet are so very joyful and thankful. Like in the case of 18-year old <a href="http://www.bradruggles.com/2010/03/08/a-father-to-the-fatherless/" target="_blank">Eliud</a>, living alone in one of the most filthy and impoverished slums in the world. In a prayer he wrote above his door and prays each day, that God loves him enough to feed him, bless him, and give him hope for the future.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.bradruggles.com/2010/03/08/a-father-to-the-fatherless/"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1330" title="eliud" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/eliud.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="212" /></a></p>
<h5>Photos by <a href="http://www.bradruggles.com/2010/03/08/a-father-to-the-fatherless/" target="_blank">Brad Ruggles</a></h5>
<p>It was realizing that in 50 years no one will care about what kind of car I drove, house I had, or clothes I wore. Who am I to be discontent because of what I &#8220;don&#8217;t have&#8221;? I am going to be honest and say that I have felt discontent too many times about things that are so meaningless, like the kind of sheets I have on my bed. Or wishing I had the money to go shopping, when I already have a closet jam packed with nice clothing.</p>
<p>This has put things in perspective for me in so many ways. Where your heart is, that&#8217;s where your treasure lies, and my heart has been places that it shouldn&#8217;t for too long.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.thisisreverb.com"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1332" title="kenya" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2010/03/kenya4.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="260" /></a></p>
<h5>Photos by <a href="http://www.thisisreverb.com" target="_blank">Ryan Detzel</a></h5>
<p>Thanks for reading if you&#8217;ve gotten this far! I know this is an unusual post for me, but I felt it was appropriate and necessary because I feel that my life, (and consequently this blog) is being transformed and I want you to be a part of it.</p>
<p>If you are interested in making an impact in a child&#8217;s life, their are 3,000 children <a href="http://www.compassion.com/sponsor_a_child/default.htm?referer=105831" target="_blank">waiting to be sponsored in Kenya</a> alone. Think about it &#8212; but not too much. Unfortunately we take more time deciding on whether or not to spend $38 a month on a life than on a pair of shoes.</p>
