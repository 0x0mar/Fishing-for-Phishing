    <div class="post-content"><p>My gals Janet + Katie with <a href="http://www.forthemakers.com">For the Makers</a> got to team up with <a href="http://www.anthropologie.com">Anthropologie</a> to create these sparkly <a href="https://www.forthemakers.com/posts/introducing-for-the-makers-anthropologie">DIY bracelet kits just for the holidays</a>. I was really excited, and alot intimidated to create illustrations for each design. I mean, it&#8217;s <em>Anthropologie</em>, and it IS my favorite store, even if I have never bought anything there.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3390" title="ftm2" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/ftm2.jpg" alt="" width="602" height="768" /></p>
<p>I&#8217;ve really come to love watercolors, and I&#8217;m so grateful for the chance to improve my skills over the last year. Thanks to Janet + Katie and Anthropologie for the opportunity! So proud of them and all their success in just a short year.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3387" title="Illustrations for Anthropologie" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/ftm1.jpg" alt="" width="446" height="625" /></p>
<blockquote><p>Everything you need to make a chic addition to your arm party is included in each sparkly kit. Instructions? Yeah we got those too- each step even has a photo. In true For the Makers fashion we made sure to include pops of pastels, hints of sparkle, and smash-ups of color. Whether it ends up in your bestie’s stocking or on your own wrist is entirely up to you. &#8211; Janet &amp; Katie</p></blockquote>
<p>They are available now in select stores and <a href="http://www.anthropologie.com/anthro/product/shopnew-accessories/26690321.jsp">online</a>.</p>

    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2012/11/11/a-full-heart/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to A Full Heart">A Full Heart</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-date">
		on		November 11, 2012,
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2012/11/11/a-full-heart/#comments">3 comments</a>
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->
    </div><!-- END .post-meta -->

    	
    
    <div class="post-content"><p>Over last weekend we successfully moved to downtown OKC &#8211; a little bit different than where <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2012/08/20/a-new-adventure/">we thought we were going to end up</a>, but I am overwhelmed with gratitude where God put us in this season. We are living in apartments across the street from <a href="http://cityrescue.org/">City Rescue mission</a>, doing life with <a href="http://refugeokc.wordpress.com/">other believers</a>, figuring out how we can serve and love our city, push back darkness and pursue Jesus. It&#8217;s been incredible and overwhelming. It&#8217;s rich and beautiful being in a community who have sacrificed to live lives of love, sharing, serving, and believing. Truly living out the Gospel and what it means to be the Church &#8211; The dream of Church that was deposited in me one year ago when I read this passage and caught it&#8217;s truth for the <em>very first time</em>:</p>
<blockquote><p>All the believers were one in heart and mind. No one claimed that any of their possessions was their own, but they shared everything they had. With great power the apostles continued to testify to the resurrection of the Lord Jesus. And God’s grace was so powerfully at work in them all that there were no needy persons among them. For from time to time those who owned land or houses sold them, brought the money from the sales and put it at the apostles’ feet, and it was distributed to anyone who had need. Acts 4:32-35</p></blockquote>
<p>It&#8217;s also a young mom&#8217;s dream &#8211; to live within steps of other moms for encouragement, support or relief. This is the way life should be. It felt so suffocating to be alone inside four walls of a house in a neighborhood, and even with all the efforts to step outside our walls, it never felt like enough. There was always <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2012/04/02/thoughts-on-community/">something lacking</a>.</p>
<p>Living in close proximity with other people, and especially other believers forces you to step outside yourself and your bubble to be accountable, refined and selfless in loving your neighbor. It&#8217;s only been 7 days, but feels like God has changed me so much already. I&#8217;m excited, and completely spilling over with the fullness of it all.</p>
<p>This is what we’re made for: We’re made to live for something bigger than ourselves, we’re made to give of ourselves for others &#8211; and hopefully by God&#8217;s grace, that&#8217;s why we are here, in a little apartment, in the broken, but beating heart of Oklahoma City.</p>
<p>&#8212;-</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3366" title="blogphoto" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/blogphoto.jpg" alt="" width="602" height="802" /></p>
<p>On a separate note, I did a little interview over at my new friend Shayla&#8217;s blog, complete with a picture of our old house that we are putting on the market to sell very soon &#8211; you can check it out here: <a href="http://www.designingalifeblog.com/2012/11/why-do-you-blog-evie-shaffer.html">Why Do You Blog?</a></p>
<p>How are you doing, friends?</p>
</div>
