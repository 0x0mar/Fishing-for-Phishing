    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">

    <div class="post-content"><p>Today is the first day I actually have felt inspired to write on this blog. It was this photo that did it.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1589" title="water" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/water.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="762" /></p>
<h6>{ found via <a href="http://poppytalk.blogspot.com/2010/07/gone-swimming.html" target="_blank">poppytalk</a> }</h6>
<p>I love this photo &#8212; the mood it creates, a story without words. The harrowing waters appear haunted and nudge the imagination of the mysteries that lie in the depths below.</p>
<p>It&#8217;s interesting how you can be going about life, seemingly mundane, and all of a sudden inspiration hits you when you weren&#8217;t expecting it.  This season of life I&#8217;m in right now, where dirty diapers from tiny bottoms and needy cries consume my day, it&#8217;s nice to know the creative part of me isn&#8217;t dead&#8230;it&#8217;s just hibernating for a while. Percolating. I&#8217;m finding myself with lots of time to think and less time to DO. So that results in a lot of ideas. And I can&#8217;t wait to share them.</p>
<p>What is inspiring you today?</p>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2010/07/05/artist-interview-leah-duncan/#comments">4 comments</a>

    <div class="post-content"><p>I&#8217;m baaaaaack! I can&#8217;t believe June is over and July is here. My goal is to start posting again on a regular basis, but if you don&#8217;t see me for a few days, it&#8217;s because my infant and toddler have worn me out!</p>
<p>I&#8217;m super excited to kick start the month interviewing an amazingly talented artist, Leah Duncan. If you are new to her work, check out her <a href="http://www.etsy.com/shop/leahduncan" target="_blank">etsy shop</a> and her <a href="http://leahduncan.com/" target="_blank">personal art site</a>.  I promise &#8212; you will get lost, everything is so beautiful! So let&#8217;s dive right in shall we?</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1576" title="leah1" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/leah1.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="155" /></p>
<p><strong>1. First, tell us a little about yourself.</strong><br />
I am a twenty-something illustrator and designer based in Austin, TX.</p>
<p><strong>2. You are a self-taught artist in illustration and design which requires a lot of discipline and self-motivation. How did you get your start in this field and what kept you motivated along the way?</strong><br />
I&#8217;ve always been a creative mind. Growing up in a small town in South Carolina, I didn&#8217;t know of anyone or even hear of anyone actually making a living as an artist so it never seemed like a sensible career path. I graduated from college with a bachelor of science in commercial printing (yes, printing presses and mechanical stuff &#8211; I learned how to weld!) for no other good reason than my love for the way ink and paper smells. Yes, I&#8217;m one of those people &#8211; a book sniffer. Because we got to design our printing projects I fell in love with graphic design on my own even though it wasn&#8217;t what I was being graded on.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1577" title="leah2" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/leah2.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="431" /></p>
<p><a href="http://www.etsy.com/listing/50011216/jack-rabbit-tea-towel" target="_blank">Jack Rabbit Tea Towel</a> // <a href="http://www.etsy.com/listing/50034997/moose-tea-towel" target="_blank">Moose Tea Towel</a></p>
<p>By the time I graduated I knew design would be a part of my work life in some form or another and I landed a job as a graphic designer. I moved from there to work for a screenprinting company, which is where I taught myself  to draw with the motivation of my very talented art director. He told me to draw for 20 minutes a day. It started out bad. It stayed bad for a while. It started getting better. Then when I moved to Austin two years ago I kept drawing and actually started to enjoy it. As for design, it&#8217;s something that has always been with me. I&#8217;ve always had an innate sense of color and space. Since my work is my livelihood the discipline and self-motivation part comes easy.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1578" title="leah3" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/leah3.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="361" /></p>
<p><a href="http://www.etsy.com/listing/50441961/my-heart-to-your-heart-limited-edition" target="_blank">My Heart to Your Heart Limited Edition</a> // <a href="http://www.etsy.com/listing/50115282/nesting-limited-edition-print-8-x-10" target="_blank">Nesting Limited Edition Print</a></p>
<p><strong>3. I know it can be hard for creative people to find their niche. How did you find your own &#8220;voice&#8221; as an artist?</strong><br />
I think it&#8217;s a lot of trial and error at times. When you end up with something you don&#8217;t want to throw away, well, you&#8217;re on your way to finding that voice.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1582" title="leah6" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/leah6.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="399" /></p>
<p><strong>4. I&#8217;m always curious to know the artists&#8217; process. Can you briefly describe yours from ideation to final product?</strong><br />
It starts with an idea that I scribble down in my sketchbook with a bic roundstick pen. From there I fill in with a pigment pen, add line work, scan, color, and repeat.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1579" title="leah4" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/leah4.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="356" /></p>
<p><a href="http://www.etsy.com/listing/49560838/cabo-removable-throw-pillow-cover" target="_blank">Cabo Removable Throw Pillow Cover</a> // <a href="http://www.etsy.com/listing/50782714/blooms-summer-scarf" target="_blank">Blooms Summer Scarf</a></p>
<p><strong>5. Is there anything in particular that fuels your creativity as an artist?</strong><br />
nature, animals, color, the little moments in life</p>
<p><strong>6. I know it’s probably hard to pick, but do you have a favorite out of the work that you’ve done?</strong><br />
I&#8217;m still quite new to this, so I think my latest drawings such as &#8220;harry had a bad feeling&#8221; are taking me to a new place. I&#8217;ve also expanded recently into textiles which has been great new territory.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1581" title="leah5" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2010/07/leah5.jpg" alt="" width="571" height="370" /></p>
<p><a href="http://www.etsy.com/listing/47408247/darling-im-leaving-stitched-and-signed?ref=v1_other_1" target="_blank">Darling I&#8217;m Leaving</a> // <a href="http://www.etsy.com/listing/48160874/harry-had-a-bad-feeling-print-8-x-10" target="_blank">Harry Had a Bad Feeling Print</a></p>
<p><strong>7. Lastly, tell us something random or quirky about yourself.</strong><br />
I like to listen to gangsta rap when I&#8217;m sewing. Gangsta rap and Etta James. : )</p>
<p>Thanks Leah!</p>
