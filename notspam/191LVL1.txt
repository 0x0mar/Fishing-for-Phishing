    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2013/01/31/diy-steel-ombre-ring/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to DIY Steel &amp; Ombre Ring">DIY Steel &#038; Ombre Ring</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

    <div class="post-content"><p>Hey everyone! I have a <a href="https://www.forthemakers.com/posts/wrapped-steel-ring-tutorial">quick tutorial up over at For the Makers today</a> on how to make a ring from dark annealed steel wire from the hardware store (3!) and embroidery thread. The thread I used was leftover ombre floss from the <a href="https://www.forthemakers.com/boxes/january-2013">January box,</a> but you can use any color that you have lying around. Cute and simple.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.forthemakers.com/posts/wrapped-steel-ring-tutorial"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3464" title="ring-final1" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/ring-final1.jpg" alt="" width="602" height="770" /></a></p>

    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2013/01/27/what-is-beauty/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to What is Beauty?">What is Beauty?</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-date">
		on		January 27, 2013,
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2013/01/27/what-is-beauty/#comments">1 comment</a>
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->
    </div><!-- END .post-meta -->

    	
    
    <div class="post-content"><p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3447" title="beauty1" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/beauty1.jpg" alt="" width="602" height="644" /></p>
<p>There are some whose idea of beauty is just too small. We tend to make snap judgments about what is beautiful, and what is not. But beauty can be strong and astringent, disturbing, shocking and dark. It can also be  easily overlooked and ignored. I am tired of our culture creating a formula for beauty and dictating it to the masses. Recognizing beauty is not about having good taste. Tastes change as we evolve as a civilization. Trends come and go. Beauty can be a constant. A sunset will always be beautiful, a new life entering the world will always be beautiful.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3448" title="beauty" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/beauty.jpg" alt="" width="602" height="803" /></p>
<p>As a designer, you learn that certain compositions, colors, lines and placement make a better design. If you stick to these basic proven principles, your art should be timeless. Contrived rules without scientific merit should always be questioned. Rules like, “Don&#8217;t wear white after Labor Day.”</p>
<p>Explore the perimeter of “good taste.” Don&#8217;t get stuck in the rut of trends and fads.  Because while I&#8217;ve found that beauty is subjective and depends on the person, it usually stays the same. If I learn to recognize on my own what is beautiful, it doesn&#8217;t change. And if I train my eye and heart to find what&#8217;s beautiful in even an ugly situation or environment, no matter what happens, that is my constant.</p>
<p>I don&#8217;t want to be known for my “good taste.” I want to be able to recognize beauty in unexpected places, and invite others to see it too. Maybe give what&#8217;s repulsive a second glance. Great art hasn&#8217;t always been in good taste. Before the avant-garde art movements of the twentieth century, it would be interesting to hear the Authority on Good Taste&#8217;s response to <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guernica_(painting)">Picasso’s Guernica</a>. In my view “good taste” can be so confining and restrictive &#8211; to the point that it isolates true beauty.<strong id="internal-source-marker_0.4053025443572551"> </strong></p>
<p>And here is the moral here. God doesn&#8217;t really have good taste when it comes to us.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3449" title="beauty2" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/beauty2.jpg" alt="" width="602" height="803" /></p>
<p>Picasso said that good taste is the enemy of great art.</p>
<p>Good taste has everything to do with being cultured and refined. Art has everything to do with being human. That’s why I love the Bible. The humans in the Bible are not very refined. And neither am I.</p>
<blockquote><p>People would always say when I was a kid and say cheer up, God loves you. I would say big deal, God loves everybody, that don’t make me special. That just proves that God ain&#8217;t got no taste. And I don’t think he does. God takes the junk of our lives and he makes the greatest art in the world out of it. If God was cultured, if he was as civilized as most Christians wish he was, we would be useless to Christianity. Because God is a wild man, and I hope that over the course of your life you encounter him. &#8211; Rich Mullins</p></blockquote>
<p>Here is what I think - <strong>things are lovely not because they are beautiful, they are beautiful because they are loved.</strong>  There is something about the things or people you love that you are attracted to and find appealing. But I think we need to enlarge the circle of what we deem beautiful. Love is the vehicle for this. Love invites us as artists to take things that would be considered without value and give it value. Art can be just as much about creative solutions for our world as it is for our own enjoyment and to the glory of God.</p>
<p>What are your thoughts?</p>
</div>

    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2013/01/04/winter/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Winter">Winter</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-date">
		on		January 4, 2013,
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2013/01/04/winter/#comments">1 comment</a>
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->
    </div><!-- END .post-meta -->

    	
    
    <div class="post-content"><p>I love winter and I hate it. Being cold is just so miserable, yet I love the cool wintery hue that the world takes on, and especially when it snows &#8211; the stillness and wonder that thickens the air and seems to freeze time itself.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3430" title="winter2" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/winter2.jpg" alt="" width="602" height="803" /></p>
<p>I&#8217;ve been knitting up some cozy gifts for loved ones. I tried my hand at making these little canvas tags that have thoughtful words stamped on them and sewing them straight on &#8211; one of those sweet little details that I love incorporating into gifts.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3432" title="winter3" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/winter3.jpg" alt="" width="602" height="803" /></p>
<p>There is a beautiful pattern for a lacy looking cowl that is just so easy and quick to knit called the <a href="http://www.ravelry.com/patterns/library/drop-stitch-cowl-3">Drop Stitch Cowl</a>. If you are a member of ravelry, you can check it out there &#8211; tis free!</p>
<p><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/winter1.jpg" rel="image_group"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-3429" title="winter1" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/winter1.jpg" alt="" width="602" height="803" /></a></p>
<p><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2012/11/11/a-full-heart/">Since moving</a>, I&#8217;m also making it a fun challenge to find natural beauty while living in the heart of the city. My mother gave me her flower presses about a year ago, and I&#8217;ve been pressing leaves like a mad woman, in fact it&#8217;s an addiction now.</p>
<p>I find God in creation. Connecting with Him in a meaningful way comes easily when I&#8217;m in nature. So being in an urban environment makes me miss, well&#8230;trees. And flowers. And just the rustic beauty of dirt and earth.</p>
<p>It is not unusual for me to be floored by leaves like the one above, especially the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stachys_byzantina">Lamb&#8217;s Ear</a> on the far right, that feels just like &#8211; a lamb&#8217;s ear. And it grows <em>from the ground</em>. Is that not just completely and utterly amazing? God&#8217;s handiwork excites and inspires me like nothing else.</p>
<blockquote><p>You know that behind the watch’s dial the hidden work of springs and gears occurs, and that the movement of the hands across the dial is caused by that hidden working. So too in terms of God’s creation, behind that creation, a hidden, secret working of God’s power and wisdom is occurring, and that only thereby do things operate as they do. This working is not an unconscious operation of a languidly propelled power, but the working of a power that is being led by three truths that fit together. First, the full and rich clarity of God’s thoughts existed in God from eternity. Second, in creation God has revealed, embedded, and embodied a rich fullness of his thoughts. And third, God created in human beings, as his image-bearers, the capacity to understand, to grasp, to reflect, and to arrange within a totality these thoughts expressed in the creation. Beauty, and beyond that, divine glory is the Spirit radiating through what appears before our eyes. -Abraham Kuyper, <em>Wisdom &amp; Wonder</em></p></blockquote>
</div>
