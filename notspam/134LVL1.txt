    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2008/01/31/postage-stamp-design/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Postage Stamp Design">Postage Stamp Design</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-date">
		on		January 31, 2008,
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2008/01/31/postage-stamp-design/#comments">0 comments</a>
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->
    </div><!-- END .post-meta -->

    	
    
    <div class="post-content"><p>I came across this flickr set of postage stamps from <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/davidmcfarline/" target="_blank">David McFarline</a>, and I really enjoyed the designs. Some of my favs are  below, but there is much to appreciate about all of them. They have been collected from various places: Bulgaria, Finland, and Brazil, just to name a few.</p>
<p><img src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2008/01/stamps.jpg" alt="Postage Stamp Design" /></p>
<p>Here is a little description about the Bulgarian stamp on the very left:</p>
<blockquote><p>This one is created for the &#8220;Banner of Peace&#8221; International Children Festival of Arts. The first run was in July 31, 1979, hence the date on the stamp. The two birds in the girl&#8217;s hair correspond to the logo of the festival &#8211; two doves in an egg shell, representing the world (also shown on the stamp).
