		<div id="post-172" class="post-172 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-art category-design category-things-of-beauty tag-art-installation tag-construkt tag-experiential-environment tag-formavision tag-marithe-francois-girbaud">
    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2008/02/29/creating-environment-with-art/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Creating Environment with Art">Creating Environment with Art</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-date">
		on		February 29, 2008,
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2008/02/29/creating-environment-with-art/#comments">0 comments</a>
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->
    </div><!-- END .post-meta -->

    	
    
    <div class="post-content"><p>I love these photos of an experiential environment built by <a href="http://www.formavision.info/www/" target="_blank">Formavision</a>, a company that  develops original art campaigns that capture one&#8217;s imagination and reinforces the brand&#8217;s vision. This particular installation was for cutting edge fashion designer duo, Marithe and Francois Girbaud.     <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2008/02/15/pattern-and-the-beauty-of-tessellations/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Pattern and the Beauty of Tessellations">Pattern and the Beauty of Tessellations</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-date">
		on		February 15, 2008,
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2008/02/15/pattern-and-the-beauty-of-tessellations/#comments">0 comments</a>
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->
    </div><!-- END .post-meta -->

    	
    
    <div class="post-content"><p>Pattern is certainly a staple of design, and tessellations are some of the most beautiful and elaborate forms of art patterns that you can find. Tessellations come from the early Greeks, and has a <a href="http://www.gvsu.edu/math/students/adk/history.html" target="_blank">rich history</a>. The famous <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M._C._Escher" target="_blank">M.C. Escher</a> is known by many as the &#8220;father of tessellations&#8221; because of the Mathematical and crystallographic aspects of his work. His 1938 work entitled <a href="http://www.tessellations.org/eschergallery10.htm" target="_blank">&#8220;Fish&#8221;</a> is one of my favorites.</p>
<p>So, my love for these beautiful patterns was recently ignited when I came across these <a href="http://flickr.com/photos/origomi/sets/72057594090171189/" target="_blank">tessellation related photo plates</a> from Owen Jones&#8217; 1853 classic book, &#8220;The Grammar of Ornament&#8221; on Flickr. [ <a href="http://veerle.duoh.com/blog/comments/pattern_inspiration/" target="_blank">Via Veerle's blog</a> ]</p>
<p><img src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2008/02/tessellations.jpg" alt="Tessellations" /></p>
<p>I hope you enjoy the beauty and vibrancy of them as much as I did!</p>
