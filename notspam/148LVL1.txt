    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2009/04/27/diy-coaster-craft/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to DIY Coaster Craft">DIY Coaster Craft</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-date">
		on		April 27, 2009,
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2009/04/27/diy-coaster-craft/#comments">8 comments</a>
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->
    </div><!-- END .post-meta -->

    	
    
    <div class="post-content"><p>I am a recovering pack rat. There are many things I keep, thinking, &#8220;someday I&#8217;ll do something with that.&#8221; Sometimes I do, sometimes I don&#8217;t. When my husband picked up these nice leather coasters that a local housing complex was using as a promotional giveaway, I tucked them away in my craft room with the intention of repurposing them. And that I did.</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-409" title="DIY Coasters" src="http://evie-s.com/news/wp-content/uploads/2009/04/diycoasters.jpg" alt="DIY Coasters" width="571" height="424" /></p>
<p>All you need for this craft are some coasters to repurpose, paper, scissors or circle cutter and modge podge.</p>
<ol>
<li>Cut circles out of decorative paper (I used vintage wallpaper) using circle cutter or scissors to fit the face of the coasters.</li>
<li>Use modge podge to adhere them to the coasters surface and let dry.</li>
<li>Seal the top using a layer of modge podge and let dry.</li>
</ol>
<p>That&#8217;s all there is to it. I may keep these for myself, but they&#8217;d also make a great gift.</p>
<p>To see full-size photos, <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/evie-s/3481254630/" target="_blank">go here</a>.</p>
