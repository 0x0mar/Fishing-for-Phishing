	<article id="post-1333" class="post-1333 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-security tag-detect-fake-sites tag-gstarshopengland tag-identify-fake-shops tag-identify-scam-website tag-scam-websites">
				<header class="entry-header">
			
						<h1 class="entry-title">
				<a href="http://blog.urlvoid.com/1333/how-to-identify-fake-shopping-websites/" rel="bookmark">How to identify fake shopping websites</a>
			</h1>
										<div class="comments-link">
					<a href="http://blog.urlvoid.com/1333/how-to-identify-fake-shopping-websites/#respond" title="Comment on How to identify fake shopping websites"><span class="leave-reply">Leave a reply</span></a>				</div><!-- .comments-link -->
					</header><!-- .entry-header -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p>When you want to buy something on Internet, you generally search for cheap clothes, or cheap shoes, but that is not always the best way to find a trusted and legit shopping website. Many scam websites are located on the top pages on Google search engine when you search for a brand name followed by the word &#8220;cheap&#8221;, &#8220;cheapest&#8221; or &#8220;wholesale&#8221;.</p>
<p>Fake shopping websites are similar to any other legit e-commerce website, with a good HTML template, with all the logos related to payments accepted, such as credit cards, paypal, etc and with logos related to (fake) trustworthiness certificates.</p>
<p>Now I will tell you few ways to identify fake shopping sites, taking as example:</p>

<div class="wp_syntax"><table><tr><td class="code"><pre class="text" style="font-family:monospace;">hxxp://gstarshopengland.com/ ---&gt; fake shopping website</pre></td></tr></table></div>

<p><a href="http://blog.urlvoid.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/fake-shopping-site-template.png"><img src="http://blog.urlvoid.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/fake-shopping-site-template.png" alt="fake-shopping-site-template" width="600" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1334" /></a></p>
<h3>1) Check the website with URLVoid</h3>
<p>Before buy something from a website, I would recommend to always check the website with our free service <a href="http://www.urlvoid.com/" target="_blank">URLVoid</a>, so the website can be analyzed with multiple scanning engines to facilitate the detection of malicious and fraudulent websites.</p>
<h3>2) Look at the prices, low prices are not always good</h3>
<p>When you see too low prices, with discounts of 50%, 55% or even more, you should become a bit suspicious. The website may promote low prices to quickly sell the fake or inexistent items, before that their website become detected as fraudulent by security software and services.</p>
<h3>3) Check the footer text and look for the company name</h3>
<p><a href="http://blog.urlvoid.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/fake-shopping-site-footer.png"><img src="http://blog.urlvoid.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/fake-shopping-site-footer.png" alt="fake-shopping-site-footer" width="620" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1338" /></a> </p>
<p>From the footer you can read this text:</p>

<div class="wp_syntax"><table><tr><td class="code"><pre class="text" style="font-family:monospace;">Copyright C 2005-2011 G Star jackets for men Sale Powered by www. gstarshopengland .com All Rights Reserved.</pre></td></tr></table></div>

<p>Is the name &#8220;G Star jackets for men Sale Powered&#8221; a legit company name ? Of course it is not. There is no reference to a legit company name, an address or a contact information. Every legitimate and trusted website should always have the name of the company located in the footer near the copyright text, with at least the company&#8217;s address or the company&#8217;s VAT/IVA ID (if in EU).</p>
<h3>4) Compare the copyright date with the domain creation date</h3>
<p>The website states it was born in 2005 with the text &#8220;Copyright 2005-2011&#8243;, but if you do a <a href="http://www.toolsvoid.com/whois-lookup" target="_blank">whois lookup</a> on the domain name, you can clearly see that the website was created on 19 August 2013, only few months ago:</p>

<div class="wp_syntax"><table><tr><td class="code"><pre class="text" style="font-family:monospace;">Updated Date: 19-aug-2013
Creation Date: 19-aug-2013
Expiration Date: 19-aug-2014</pre></td></tr></table></div>

<h3>5) Avoid buying clothes from young websites</h3>
<p>With a <a href="http://www.toolsvoid.com/whois-lookup" target="_blank">whois lookup</a>, make sure to always check the domain creation date, if the domain name was registered only few months ago, I would recommend you to not buy anything because there are not enough details to tell if the website is a legit website or a fraudulent website.</p>
<h3>6) Check who is the owner of the website</h3>
<p>From the whois data, you can see that the website was registered in Beijin (China):</p>

<div class="wp_syntax"><table><tr><td class="code"><pre class="text" style="font-family:monospace;">Registrar: BEIJING INNOVATIVE LINKAGE TECHNOLOGY LTD. DBA DNS.COM.CN</pre></td></tr></table></div>

<p>The name servers are also Chinese (.CN):</p>

<div class="wp_syntax"><table><tr><td class="code"><pre class="text" style="font-family:monospace;">Name Server: NS13.DNS.COM.CN
Name Server: NS14.DNS.COM.CN</pre></td></tr></table></div>

<p>The organization name is also Chinese:</p>

<div class="wp_syntax"><table><tr><td class="code"><pre class="text" style="font-family:monospace;">Organisation Name.... yanfang li
Organisation Address. xiantaoshishahuzhenfenglecun1zu55hao
Organisation Address.
Organisation Address. Jingzhou
Organisation Address. 433019
Organisation Address. HB
Organisation Address. CN</pre></td></tr></table></div>

<p>The email and telephone number of the website owner are also Chinese:</p>

<div class="wp_syntax"><table><tr><td class="code"><pre class="text" style="font-family:monospace;">Tech Email........... xiandailihao@163.com
Tech Phone........... +86.7282640476
Tech Fax............. +86.7282640476</pre></td></tr></table></div>

<p>Personally, I would not buy G-Star clothes from a website that was registered in China, I would prefer to buy them from the official store or from other stores near the place where I live, so I can more easily make a telephone call to the owner or visit their shop directly in case of a problem.</p>
<h3>7) Make sure the website has HTTPS support</h3>
<p><a href="http://blog.urlvoid.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/fake-shopping-site-no-ssl.png"><img src="http://blog.urlvoid.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/fake-shopping-site-no-ssl.png" alt="fake-shopping-site-no-ssl" width="800" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1339" /></a></p>
<p>When you try to buy an item, if you go to the checkout, you can see that there is no secure connection HTTPS, every legitimate e-commerce website should have HTTPS support when the user is supposed to insert sensitive information or credit card details. I would never buy something from a website that has no HTTPS support.</p>
<h3>8) Visit the about or contacts page to find valid contact information</h3>
<p><a href="http://blog.urlvoid.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/fake-shopping-site-no-contacts.png"><img src="http://blog.urlvoid.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/fake-shopping-site-no-contacts.png" alt="fake-shopping-site-no-contacts" width="800" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1348" /></a></p>
<p>As you can see from the above image, the website has no information about how to contact the shop, such as a telephone number, an email address, or the company&#8217;s address. I would never buy something from a shopping website that has only a contact form, I prefer to have a phone number, a valid email address and a valid company&#8217;s address to verify. Take in mind that most scam websites use public email addresses, such as @gmail.com, @yahoo.com, @163.com, @hotmail.com, etc. A legitimate website should use the website&#8217;s email address, for example info@website.com.</p>
<h3>9) Analyze the domain name string</h3>
<p>If you see a website that has the domain name similar to: buy-cheap-shoes.com, or buycheapshoes.com, or super-cheap-shoes.com, or jordanairmaxshop.com, or wholesalenikeshoes.com, gstarshoppingengland.com or similar, you should avoid buying something from there. A legit website, should not contain the brand name in the domain name and should not contain the word &#8220;cheap&#8221;, &#8220;cheapest&#8221;, &#8220;wholesale&#8221;, etc.</p>
<h3>10) Make sure the English language is correct</h3>
<p>Even if a legitimate website may contain grammar errors, some fake shopping sites may have a lot of grammar errors, so make sure to read few pages and check the language grammar, if you see too many errors, you may become a bit suspicious on the trustworthiness of the website.</p>
<h3>11) Google is your friend to search information about a website</h3>
<p>You can search on Google more information about a suspicious website, such as you can search the telephone number, the email address, the website owner name, the organization name, or simply search if other users had a bad experience with that website. You can also search the IP address on Google, to see if there are other useful information about that IP.</p>
<h3>12) Analyze the websites hosted in the same IP address</h3>
<p><a href="http://blog.urlvoid.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/urlvoid-ip-address-websites.png"><img src="http://blog.urlvoid.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/urlvoid-ip-address-websites.png" alt="urlvoid-ip-address-websites" width="644" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1354" /></a></p>
<p>If a website comes up as clean on <a href="http://www.urlvoid.com/" target="_blank">URLVoid</a>, you can always analyze if there are other websites hosted in the same IP address, that are malicious or detected by other scanning engines. URLVoid offer the possibility to see how many websites are hosted in an IP address, but you can also search on Google to see if another website has more information.</p>
<h3>13) Analyze the website&#8217;s IP address with IPVoid</h3>
<p>I would recommend to scan the IP address of the website with <a href="http://www.ipvoid.com/" target="_blank">IPVoid</a>, a free service used to better know if an IP address has been blacklisted by anti-spam services or if it has participated in illicit activities.</p>

	<article id="post-1305" class="post-1305 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-spam-2 tag-casino-spam tag-google-translate tag-pharma-spam tag-yolasite-spam">

						<h1 class="entry-title">
				<a href="http://blog.urlvoid.com/1305/google-translate-used-by-spammers-to-bypass-anti-spam-filters/" rel="bookmark">Google Translate used by spammers to bypass Anti-Spam filters</a>
			</h1>
										<div class="comments-link">
					<a href="http://blog.urlvoid.com/1305/google-translate-used-by-spammers-to-bypass-anti-spam-filters/#respond" title="Comment on Google Translate used by spammers to bypass Anti-Spam filters"><span class="leave-reply">Leave a reply</span></a>				</div><!-- .comments-link -->
					</header><!-- .entry-header -->

				<div class="entry-content">
			<p>Google Translate is a free service created by Google that translates any web page, content or document from native language to a language specified by the user that is using the service. We have noticed that some spam messages contain links to websites that use the service Google Translate to translate their page content, but those links are used to promote fraudulent pharmaceutical products, and they seem to use Google Translate to masquerade the malicious website.</p>
<p>In short, when you translate an URL with Google Translate, it appends the URL of the web page in the HTTP query string, but the initial domain name remains translate.googleusercontent.com, so the anti-spam filters may be bypassed because the URL of Google Translate is classified as legitimate.</p>
<p>To get a better idea about what I am talking about, check this image:</p>
<p><a href="http://blog.urlvoid.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/translate-google-used-for-spam.png"><img src="http://blog.urlvoid.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/10/translate-google-used-for-spam.png" alt="translate-google-used-for-spam" width="626" height="243" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1306" /></a></p>
<p>We have extracted some URLs from the spam messages and they are all subdomains of yolasite(dot)com, they are used to promote selling of fake pharmaceutical products and subscriptions to fraudulent casino websites:</p>

<div class="wp_syntax"><table><tr><td class="code"><pre class="text" style="font-family:monospace;">hxxp:// myonlinestore1. yolasite.com/shop
hxxp:// onlineshop63. yolasite.com/shop
hxxp:// onlinecasino27. yolasite.com/casino2</pre></td></tr></table></div>

<p>Never click on links that start with the domain “translate.googleusercontent.com”, because they may use Google Translate to translate a malicious website and exploit vulnerabilities in your web browser or other applications installed in your system (such as Adobe Flash, PDF Readers, Java) to infect your PC.</p>
<p>If you want to translate a website, you should visit directly with your browser the website of Google Translate and type the URL that you want to translate. Avoid clicking on links related to Google Translate, present in emails or in other unknown websites.</p>

<p><a href="http://blog.urlvoid.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/pshishing-paypal-notice-of-policy-update.png"><img src="http://blog.urlvoid.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/pshishing-paypal-notice-of-policy-update.png" alt="pshishing-paypal-notice-of-policy-update" width="638" height="403" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1283" /></a></p>

<div class="wp_syntax"><table><tr><td class="code"><pre class="text" style="font-family:monospace;">hxxp://lnko.in/bhqr</pre></td></tr></table></div>

<p>The user is redirected to these malicious links (in order):</p>

<div class="wp_syntax"><table><tr><td class="code"><pre class="text" style="font-family:monospace;">hxxp://107.6.59.96/recordings/misc/
hxxp://107.6.59.96/recordings/misc/3d1e2032ae804fac6c085a5f6b7a8b3a/
hxxp://107.6.59.96/recordings/misc/3d1e2032ae804fac6c085a5f6b7a8b3a/personal/
