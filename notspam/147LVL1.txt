			Monthly Archives: <span>March 2009</span>	</h2>	
		<div id="post-373" class="post-373 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-art category-things-of-beauty tag-book-art tag-book-mobile tag-book-sculptures tag-book-sphere tag-paper-sculpture">
    <h2 class="post-title"><a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2009/03/25/book-art-and-sculpture/" rel="bookmark" title="Permalink to Book Art and Sculpture">Book Art and Sculpture</a></h2>

    <div class="post-meta">
	    <span class="post-author">
		    by		    <a href="http://evie-s.com/news/author/evies./" title="Posts by evie s." rel="author">evie s.</a>	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-date">
		on		March 25, 2009,
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->

	    <span class="post-comments">
		<a href="http://evie-s.com/news/2009/03/25/book-art-and-sculpture/#comments">4 comments</a>
	    </span><!-- .post-author -->
    </div><!-- END .post-meta -->

    	
    
    <div class="post-content"><p>One of my favorite things is to take something already existing and repurpose it. Artists have such a knack for this. American philosopher, <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francis_Schaeffer" target="_blank">Francis Schaeffer</a>, says that since we are made in the image of God, we can be co-creators with Him. I love that. I firmly believe that everyone is creative, and has the capacity to create&#8230;something. Anyway, that was a rabbit trail. What I really want to talk about are these fabulous vintage books that have been transformed into beautiful art.</p>
<p>From left to right:</p>
