<h1>Eating Psychology: The Movement</h1>						<a id="dd_start"></a><style>
#marg {
margin: 0 0 10px;
}
.article ul li {list-style: disc;}
.article ul li:before { background: none;}
.article h1 { text-align: center; color: white; background-image: url(/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/headerbackground.jpg); background-size: cover; padding: 13px; border-radius: 5px; text-shadow: none;}
</style>
<p><img class="alignnone pretty" title="The Movement" src="http://psychologyofeating.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/10/themovement_header.jpg" alt="The Movement" width="100%" /><br />
<strong><span style="color: #693f8c;">Dear Friends &#8211; After teaching, consulting and writing for the last 3 decades, I’m honored to introduce to you a wonderful, life-changing educational opportunity with a new online health coach training. The Institute for the Psychology of Eating is at the forefront of a powerful, new, holistic nutritional healing movement that’s changing the way we understand food, weight, body image, holistic nutrition, and health. Our students come from all walks of life and many countries with this one common goal – to learn a better way to heal our relationship with food, body, planet, and soul.</span></strong></p>
<p><img class="alignright pretty" title="savor" src="http://psychologyofeating.com/wp-content/uploads/iStock_000009660618Small-300x199.jpg" alt="" width="295" height="194" />For far too long, people have struggled without real answers to these profoundly important questions:</p>
<ul>
<li>How can I lose weight in a sustainable and loving way?</li>
<li>How can I stop punishing my body with food and learn to nourish it and feel free?</li>
<li>How can I know which foods are right for me to eat amidst all the conflicting nutritional viewpoints put forth by the experts?</li>
</ul>
<p>As I look around at all the chaos and confusion in the field of nutrition, I see that we’re at a profound crossroad. Medical science has finally recognized the important role of diet in optimal health, yet something is clearly missing. Obesity, overeating, pain around body image, emotional challenges with food, digestive ailments, fatigue and diet-related health concerns are with us more than ever. People have access to all kinds of nutrition information, but need to search long and hard for true healing wisdom.</p>
<blockquote>
<h3>Perhaps you’re aware of some of these eye-opening statistics:</h3>
<ul>
<ul>
<li>9 out of 10 women are unhappy with their weight, shape, or looks</li>
</ul>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li>8 out of 10 women are on a diet</li>
<li>40% of 9-year-old females have been on a diet</li>
<li>Over 98% of people who lose weight by dieting gain it back within a year</li>
<li>Over 65% of people complain of digestive issues</li>
<li>Over 50% of people complain of constant fatigue</li>
</ul>
</blockquote>
<p>Clearly, our collective approach to nutritional health and happiness isn’t working.</p>
<h3>It’s time for a fresh start, and new way of seeing things.</h3>
<p id="marg">IPE is at the forefront of a new movement that’s re-uniting the psychology of eating with the science of nutrition. We understand the powerful and intimate connection between women and food. We acknowledge the need for a holistic approach to weight and its loss.
